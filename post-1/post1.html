AI and Content Creation

There is currently a rising trend for Artificial Intelligence in our digital landscape. The popularity of generative AI programs such as ChatGBT and MidJourney is contributing to the rapid growth of generative AI. Developers depend on AI to finish their work faster. Even large companies are also taking part in this new trend and are starting to implement AI in their software. A great example of this can be shown through companies like Google and Microsoft, who are currently trying to see if they can improve their search engines by applying AI. Thanks to generative AI, it can quickly finish work faster and help produce new content. After hearing about all the wonders AI can do, I was wondering about its potential use in my field. To give a brief background about myself, I am currently studying Digital Media and Design and my concentration is in Web Interactivity and Design. I aspire to become a front-end Developer and Designer. Since I work a lot around code and web development, there are several ways that I can use AI in my workflow. One method is that it can help me by writing code. Thanks to AI’s generative nature, It saves a ton of time by producing code more quickly. This eliminates the need for writing code manually, and allows me to concentrate on other important tasks. Additionally, it can quickly and efficiently test and debug code. This can save me from hours of stress and frustration. Another potential use for Generative AI in my workflow is that it can be used to create designs and images. Applications like DALL-E, allow users to generate images through a written prompt. I won’t have to worry about coming up with a fresh, interesting design, and will save me a lot of time. Although there are a lot of benefits to the use of AI, it does bring up some ethical concerns. I oppose the use of AI in the arts since it relies on work from artists. It violates the artist’s intellectual property rights and steals away potential job opportunities for artists. Because of this, artists are starting to impose copyright lawsuits against generative AI tools. AI tools such as Midjourney are facing lawsuits for allegedly violating copyright laws by stealing artists’ work and using it to train their AI without permission (The Verge). I understand that using AI sounds intriguing due to its ability to quickly produce content, but it also raises concerns such as copyright infringement and the possibility of taking away jobs from people. While I am not entirely opposed to the use of AI, as it saves so much time when it comes to code and development, it does undermine the authenticity of art. The uniqueness and originality of art produced by humans are being replaced by AI-generated art. Whether AI art can truly be recognized as “art” and of itself is a difficult question for many. As for my response, AI-generated art should never be accepted as authentic art.
